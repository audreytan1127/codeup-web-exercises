National Park FAQ!

Yellowstone National Park

Located in three states (Wyoming, Montana, and Idaho) it displays small and large geysers due to the volcanic hot spot it is atop.

Yosemite National Park

The largest sequoia trees are located within this national park in the Sierra Nevada Mountains

Grand Teton National Park

This large national park is linked to Yellowstone National Park and displays large mountain ranges.

Zion Canyon National Park

With it's steep red cliffs, a river flows into the Emerald Pools which is home to waterfalls and hanging gardens.

Hawaii Volcanoes National Park

Within the heart of this park are two active volcanoes and is home to the largest lava dome volcano in the world.

Mount Rainier National Park

South of Seattle, there are mountain ranges, meadows, and nearby volcanoes to view.

Theodore Roosevelt National Park

Where the Great Plains and the Badlands meet to create a sanctuary for many wild animals, such as elk, bison, and prarie dogs.

Redwood National and State Parks

Housed in California, projecting California to be the top contender for the most National Parks in one state, at 8!

Great Smoky Mountains National Park

Between North Carolina and Tennessee, lush wildflowers bloom year-round in this mountainous national park.

Gates of Arctic National Park and Preserve

This is the northernmost National Park in the United States, entirely north of the Arctic Circle.

Big Bend National Park

  • Named after the Rio Grande's largest bend in its river.
  • Over 450 bird species, more than any other national park.
  • There are a recorded 31 species of snakes residing in the park.
  • It is one of the most remote parks in the United States.

Arches National Park

  • Densest concentration of natural stone arches in the World.
  • There are many balanced rocks.
  • Winter time is the quietest and best time to explore this 76,000+ acre park.
  • The tallest arch is at a staggering 144 feet.

Grand Canyon National Park

  • The Grand Canyon is bigger than Rhode Island.
  • The park is over 1.2 million acres large.
  • The canyon goes a mile deep, is 277 miles long, and 18 miles wide.
  • The Grand Canyon can affect the weather in surrounding areas.
